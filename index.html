Approach

My playbook starts with people. The channels come after.

Every audience is different. Developers distrust outreach. Crypto users need social proof. Fintech consumers in Tier 2 India need education before a product pitch. The surface changes every time. The foundation doesn't.

At Mudrex, I walked into offices unannounced to talk to real users. I built a student community of 5,000+ that eventually ran itself because the ambassadors believed in it. That taught me the most important thing I know: if people feel ownership, they become your distribution.

At OLake, I took that same principle into a completely different market. Developers don't want to be sold to, so I built an education-led engine: 15 webinars and 7 global events in 6 months, featuring practitioners from Google, Meta, Netflix, and Snowflake. Zero budget. All through relationships.

At Money Club, it was Tier 2 India. I travelled to understand what people actually need from financial products. The collateral I designed from those insights generated 100+ leads at a single event.

"The playbook personalises on top. The first principles stay the same: understand the pain deeply enough that the solution feels obvious."

Every GTM engine I've built started the same way: conversations before campaigns. I get into the communities, the Slack groups, the events, the Discord servers where my audience actually talks. I listen first. Then I build the system.

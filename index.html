Approach

I tried the standard playbook first. It didn't work.

Cold calls, email campaigns, paid ads. With developer audiences, they actively backfire.

So I build something different: education-led ecosystems where the community does the selling. Events with practitioners. Content that teaches. Communities that sustain themselves.

"I don't run campaigns. I build systems. The kind that keep working after I stop pushing."

It starts the same way every time: 100+ conversations before a single landing page. Events, Slack groups, Discord, Reddit, LinkedIn. The market tells you what to build if you ask the right questions.

About

A bit about me.

I studied economics, taught students, and ended up building GTM engines for technical products. Every step made the next one possible.

Economics taught me how incentives actually work. Teaching taught me how to explain complex things simply. Both turned out more useful in tech than any technical degree.

I've worked across crypto, fintech, and data infrastructure. Each time building from zero, each time in a market where traditional playbooks didn't apply.

What excites me now: AI-native companies where the product is brilliant but the GTM treats developers like enterprise buyers. The companies that win won't be the ones with the best models. They'll be the ones that earn trust first.
